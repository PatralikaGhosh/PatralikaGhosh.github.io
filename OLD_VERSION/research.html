Analysis of an Electrocardiogram

I was an Undergraduate Research Assistant at the Neuroergonomics Laboratory at Texas A&M. There were many research projects that
were going on. I was on the research project that tried to quantify trust in a multi-human robot team in a first responder situation.
There were experiments that were conducted and there was a lot of data that was analyzed. I was on the team that was analyzing an
electrocardiogram of two people to find cardiac synchrony between them. It was a team project and the programming languages that
were used was Python and R. A lesser known Python library that was used is the Neurokit Library. We also used RHRV and CRQA libraries
to extract features that helped our analysis. The scientific instrument we used for data collection is Actiheart. The method of analysis
that was used was Cross-Recurrence Quantification Analysis.

Black Jack

This project is still in progress.
It is coded in Python and I have been working Tkinter currently.
Blackjack, also known as 21, is a popular card game played in casinos around the world.
The game is relatively simple to learn but involves strategic decision-making.
The primary objective of Blackjack is to beat the dealer by having a hand value as
close to 21 as possible without exceeding it or "busting." Alternatively, you win if
the dealer busts while you do not.
